Key Insights

Data-driven findings from the IBM HR Analytics dataset

⏰

Overtime is the #1 Attrition Driver

Employees who work overtime have ~3× higher attrition rate compared to those who don't. This is the strongest single predictor in both the Random Forest and SHAP analysis.

~30% vs ~10%
Attrition rate: Overtime vs No Overtime
💰

Low Salary = High Flight Risk

Employees earning below $3,000/month show significantly elevated attrition. The median salary of employees who left is considerably lower than those who stayed.

$4,787 vs $6,833
Median income: Left vs Stayed
💍

Single Employees Leave More

Single employees have the highest attrition rate among all marital groups. Married employees are the most stable, likely due to financial commitments and lifestyle stability.

Single > Divorced > Married
Attrition rate by marital status
🏢

Sales Has Highest Attrition

The Sales department consistently shows the highest attrition rate, driven by high-pressure targets, frequent travel, and competitive job market for sales professionals.

Sales > HR > R&D
Attrition rate by department
📅

Early Tenure = Highest Risk

Employees with 0–2 years at the company are most likely to leave. Attrition drops significantly after the 3-year mark, suggesting a critical retention window.

0–2 years is critical
Onboarding & early retention focus needed
✈️

Frequent Travel Increases Risk

Employees who travel frequently for business show noticeably higher attrition than those who rarely travel or don't travel at all — highlighting work-life balance concerns.

Frequently > Rarely > Non-Travel
Attrition rate by travel frequency

HR Recommendations

01

Overtime Policy

Limit consistent overtime to <15% of workforce. Introduce comp-time policies. Overtime workers show 3× the attrition risk.

02

Salary Review

Benchmark compensation especially for Job Level 1–2 roles. Income below $3k/month correlates strongly with attrition.

03

Early Onboarding

Focus retention efforts in years 0–2. Assign mentors, structured 30/60/90 day plans, and early career pathing.

04

Sales Department

Review target-setting, commission structures, and travel frequency for Sales. This department has the highest flight risk.

05

Satisfaction Surveys

Employees with Job Satisfaction ≤ 2 are at elevated risk. Quarterly pulse surveys can catch dissatisfaction early.

06

Use the Risk Predictor

Run the ML risk predictor monthly. Focus 1-on-1s with High Risk employees before they actively seek alternatives.
